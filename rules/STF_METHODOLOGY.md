# STF Methodology: Start To Finish

**Version:** 1.0  
**Created:** February 25, 2026  
**Author:** Project Nautilus

---

## The Philosophy

**STF = Start To Finish**

Like a master cabinet maker building cabinets for an entire house, you must first study ALL the architect's drawings—every elevation, every measurement, every detail—to familiarize yourself with every step involved in transforming raw lumber and plywood into a finished product that fits within a tolerance of 1/32 of an inch.

**If you don't understand the complete journey from start to finish BEFORE you begin work, mistakes will be made. And those mistakes compound.**

---

## Application to Diagnostics

When diagnosing a pinball machine issue:

1. **Understand the FULL diagnostic path** before taking the first action
2. **Know where you're going** (the end state) before you start checking components
3. **See all the steps** from symptom identification through to verified repair
4. **Anticipate compounding errors** that happen when you skip understanding the complete picture

This means:
- Don't just react to the immediate symptom
- Don't jump to component replacement without seeing the full chain
- Don't diagnose in isolation—understand how each part relates to the whole system
- Map the entire troubleshooting journey FIRST, then execute with precision

---

## The Three Stages (Within STF)

The diagnostic progression uses cabinet making as the metaphor:

### STRAIGHT = The Carcass (Foundation)
Physical/mechanical checks. If the carcass isn't square, the finish work won't matter.

### TRUE = The Alignment (Validation)  
Electrical validation and measurements. Confirm the foundation is square before proceeding.

### FLUSH = The Finish (Execution)
Root cause repair and manual-specific work. Only after foundation and alignment are verified.

**But remember:** These three stages are steps WITHIN the Start-To-Finish methodology. You must understand all three stages and how they connect BEFORE you begin STRAIGHT.

---

## Why This Matters

**Compounding Mistakes:**
- Replace a coil without checking power supply → coil burns out again
- Fix a switch without understanding the circuit → creates new problems downstream
- Jump to board work without mechanical checks → waste time and money

**STF Prevents This:**
- See the whole system first
- Understand dependencies and relationships
- Execute with precision because you know where you're going
- Build tolerance into your diagnostic process (1/32" accuracy mindset)

---

## The Cabinet Maker's Discipline

A cabinet maker who doesn't study the complete drawings first will:
- Cut pieces to wrong dimensions
- Miss critical joinery details
- Create cascading fit problems
- Waste materials and time
- Deliver a product that doesn't fit

A diagnostic technician who doesn't understand Start-To-Finish will:
- Misdiagnose the root cause
- Replace wrong components
- Create new problems while "fixing" old ones
- Waste the user's time and money
- Leave the machine still broken

---

**Remember: Study the architect's drawings. Know the journey from raw materials to finished product. Then execute with cabinet maker precision.**
